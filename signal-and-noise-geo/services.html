Content Optimization

Content optimization restructures your existing content to maximize AI citation probability. This is not content writing. This is content engineering: applying structural patterns that AI models evaluate when selecting content to cite.

We focus on four structural properties that correlate most strongly with AI citation frequency: passage self-containment, answer-first structure, fact density, and heading hierarchy. Each optimization is measurable through re-auditing.

Optimization techniques

  • 1. Passage restructuring: Breaking content into 20-80 word self-contained passages that make sense without surrounding context. Each passage should be independently citable by an AI model.
  • 2. Answer-first rewriting: Moving the key fact to the beginning of each section and paragraph. AI models preferentially extract content from opening sentences.
  • 3. Fact density improvement: Increasing the ratio of verifiable claims, statistics, and specific data points per passage. Higher fact density correlates with higher citation probability.
  • 4. Heading hierarchy correction: Ensuring proper H1-H6 nesting with headings that function as standalone questions or statements, enabling AI models to index content by topic.

Structured Data Strategy

Structured data tells AI crawlers what your content is about in a machine-readable format. Signal & Noise GEO implements comprehensive schema markup tailored to your content types and business model.

We implement and validate Organization, Article, FAQPage, HowTo, Product, Service, and BreadcrumbList schemas. We also configure llms.txt files, optimize robots.txt for AI crawler access, and build comprehensive sitemaps.
